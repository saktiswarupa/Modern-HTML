Ans 2 >>>>...HTML entities are special codes or sequences of characters used to represent reserved characters, symbols, or characters that have
 special meaning in HTML and need to be displayed as text rather than interpreted as code. Here are five commonly used HTML entities:

&lt; - Represents the less-than sign ("<"). This entity is used when you want to display a less-than sign without it being interpreted as the opening tag of an HTML element.

&gt; - Represents the greater-than sign (">"). Similar to &lt;, this entity is used to display a greater-than sign as text rather than as the closing tag of an HTML element.

&amp; - Represents the ampersand character ("&"). This entity is used when you want to display an ampersand character without it being interpreted as the beginning of an HTML entity.

&quot; - Represents a double quotation mark ("). This entity is used to display double quotation marks within attributes of HTML elements or when you want to display them as text.

&apos; - Represents a single quotation mark ('). While not as commonly used as  &quot;, this entity is used to display single quotation marks within attributes or as text.


Ans 3 >>>...Accessibility in the context of web development refers to the practice of designing and building websites and web applications in a way
 that ensures they can be used and understood by people with disabilities.
 It aims to make digital content and services accessible to all, regardless of their physical or cognitive abilities. 

Inclusivity and Equal Access: Accessible websites ensure that people with disabilities have equal access to information and services online

Expanded Audience: Creating accessible websites broadens your audience. This isn't limited to people with disabilities; it also benefits elderly users, people using older technology or
 slower internet connections, and those in noisy environments

 Search Engine Optimization (SEO): Search engines, like Google, rank accessible websites higher in search results because they consider accessibility as a factor in their algorithms. 
 Improved SEO can increase your website's visibility and reach.

 0egal Compliance: Many countries have laws requiring websites to be accessible, avoiding legal issues and
promoting equal access.


Ans4 >>>...Use Semantic HTM0 Elements: Using semantic HTML elements helps improve accessibility by providing
meaningful structure to your content. Semantic elements like <header>, <nav>, <main>, <article>, and <footer>
convey the purpose of different sections to assistive technologies, making it easier for users with disabilities to
navigate and understand the content.

Provide Descriptive Text and Alternative Text (Alt Text):
For images, videos, and other non-text content, it's crucial to provide descriptive text or alternative text (alt text) that conveys the content or function of the element. 
Alt text is especially important for users who rely on screen readers.

Implement Keyboard Accessibility:
Ensure that all interactive elements on your web page, such as links, buttons, and form controls, are accessible
 via keyboard navigation. This is crucial for users who cannot use a mouse or other pointing devices
